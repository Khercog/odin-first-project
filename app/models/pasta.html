Pasta

There's nothing like classic spaghetti and meat sauce to bring people together around the dinner table. This homemade spaghetti sauce with ground beef recipe will satisfy all your comfort food cravings.

What you are going to need:

  • 1 pound ground beef - You can use either green or brown lentils for this stew.
  • 1 medium onion, chopped- We use a delicious combination of celery (with some of the leaves), carrot, onion, leeks and garlic for this soup.
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced - We use extra-virgin olive oil to saute the aromatics with.
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced- Added kale, sweet potato and red potato make this stew completely satisfying.
  • 1 (28 ounce) can diced tomatoes - Cut a few tomatoes in pieces (or use a can of tomateos)
  • 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce- This stew is perfectly seasoned with cumin, onion powder, cinnamon, salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste - Helps to disolves vegetables faster.
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Spaghetti

How it's made

This is one of those easy one-pot meals that comes together in about 40 minutes start-to-finish.

  • Combine ground beef, onion, garlic, and green pepper in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook and stir until meat is browned and crumbly and vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain grease.
  • Stir diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste into the pan. Season with oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer spaghetti sauce for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
